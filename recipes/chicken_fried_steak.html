Chicken Fried Steak

Easy Chicken Fried Steak Recipe

Breaded and deep-fried beef cutlets are known as chicken fried steak because of the similarity in cooking method to fried chicken.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups shortening
  • 1 pound boneless beef top loin
  • 1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour, divided
  • ¼ teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 quart milk

Steps

  1. Heat 1/2 inch shortening in a large, heavy skillet to 365 degrees F (185 degrees C).
  2. While shortening is heating, cut beef crosswise into 4 equal cutlets. Pound each cutlet thinly with a moistened mallet or the side of a cleaver.
  3. Mix together 1 cup flour and garlic powder in a shallow bowl. Beat together buttermilk, egg, salt, and pepper in a second shallow bowl.
  4. Dip cutlets in flour mixture, turning to coat evenly. Dip in egg mixture, then in flour mixture once more.
  5. Cook cutlets, one at a time, in hot shortening until golden brown, turning once. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate.
  6. Drain grease, reserving 1/2 cup drippings in the skillet.
  7. Blend remaining 1/4 cup flour into reserved drippings to form a paste. Cook and stir over medium heat, gradually adding milk until desired consistency is reached.
  8. For thicker gravy, add less milk; for thinner gravy, stir in more. Season gravy with salt and pepper.
  9. Serve over fried cutlets.
